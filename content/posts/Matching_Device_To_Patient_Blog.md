Choosing the appropriate pacing mode for a patient receiving a Cardiovascular Implantable Electronic Device (CIED) is a critical clinical decision. The right mode maximizes cardiac output and ensures atrioventricular (AV) synchrony, while the wrong mode can lead to pacemaker (PM) syndrome or hemodynamic deterioration. 

 Understanding the precise indications and contraindications for each pacing mode is essential for ensuring accurate data handling and optimal patient outcomes.

## Single-Chamber Ventricular Pacing (VVI & VVIR)

Ventricular pacing is primarily utilized when atrial pacing is either impossible or clinically inappropriate. 

* **VVIR (Rate-Responsive):** The generally agreed-upon indication is for patients with fixed atrial arrhythmias (such as atrial fibrillation or atrial flutter) accompanied by symptomatic bradycardia. 
* **VVI:** This mode is used only in the absence of rate responsive in the device.
* **Contraindications:** Both VVI and VVIR are strictly contraindicated in patients with known PM syndrome, those who experience hemodynamic deterioration with ventricular pacing, or those who have a hemodynamic need for dual-chamber pacing. Furthermore, standard VVI is contraindicated if a chronotropically incompetent (CI) patient would benefit from rate response.

## Single-Chamber Atrial Pacing (AAI & AAIR)

Atrial-based modes are ideal when the patient's AV conduction is intact.

* **Indications:** Both AAI and AAIR are indicated for symptomatic bradycardia resulting from Sinus Node Dysfunction (SND), provided AV conduction is proven normal. AAI is used for chronotropically competent (CC) patients, while AAIR is tailored for chronotropically incompetent (CI) patients.
* **Contraindications:** Avoid these modes if there is a hemodynamic need for dual-chamber pacing, or if SND is accompanied by AV block (spontaneous or during testing). They are also contraindicated in the unlikely event that adequate atrial sensing cannot be attained.

## Ventricular Pacing with Atrial Sensing (VDD & VDDR)

* **Indications:** These modes are indicated for congenital AV block, or AV block where sinus node function is proven normal. VDDR is selected over VDD if there is a potential need for ventricular rate-adaptive pacing.
* **Contraindications:** Do not use if the patient has SND (alone or accompanying the AV block), if adequate atrial sensing cannot be attained, or if the AV block is accompanied by a paroxysmal supraventricular tachycardia.

## Dual-Chamber Pacing (DDD & DDDR)

Dual-chamber pacing is often the standard for maximizing AV synchrony and cardiac output.

* **Indications:** DDD is indicated for AV block and SND in CC patients, inactive patients, or those who need AV synchrony to maximize cardiac output. It is also used to correct previous PM syndrome caused by VVI(R) pacing. DDDR is indicated for similar conditions in CI patients.
* **Controversial Uses:** DDD and DDDR may be considered for any rhythm disturbance where atrial sensing and capture are possible (excluding AF or atrial flutter), potentially to minimize future AF, reduce morbidity, and improve survival.
* **Contraindications:** These modes are firmly contraindicated in the presence of chronic AF, atrial flutter, a giant inexcitable atrium, or other frequent paroxysmal supraventricular tachyarrhythmias. They should also be avoided when adequate atrial sensing cannot be achieved. *(Note: DDI and DDIR are generally not preimplantation modes of choice).*

Proper mode selection guarantees that implantable technologies deliver the intended clinical benefits while minimizing patient risk.
